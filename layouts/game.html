RULES

In baccarat, both the player and the banker receive two cards at the beginning of the game. The player has an opportunity to bet on player's win, banker's win or tie. To change the bet player should use ON button on the bottom of the screen

In baccarat, cards have a point value: the 2 through 9 cards in each suit are worth face value (in points); the 10, jack, queen, and king have no point value (i.e. are worth zero); aces are worth 1 point.

Hands are valued according to the units digit of the sum of their constituent cards. For example, a hand consisting of 2 and 3 is worth 5, but a hand consisting of 6 and 7 is worth 3 (i.e., the 3 being the units digit in the combined points total of 13). The highest possible hand value in baccarat is therefore nine.

If the player has an initial total of 5 or less, they draw a third card. If the player has an initial total of 6 or 7, they stand.
